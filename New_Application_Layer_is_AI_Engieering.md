# **New Application Layer is AI Engineering**

## **Executive Overview**

Core thesis is that "AI Engineering" is the legitimate successor to traditional web development, fundamentally changing both *how* software is built and *what* kind of software is produced.

---

### **1\. The Economic Impact on Software Engineering**

A primary concern addressed is the fear of AI replacing software engineers. We believe that the demand for software engineers will actually increase.

* **The Elasticity of Software Demand**: The software market represents an elastic economic experiment. As AI makes it cheaper and faster to produce software, the total volume of software demanded and created will scale proportionally.  
* **The SaaS Apocalypse ("SaaS-pocalypse")**: Companies are increasingly choosing to "make" rather than "buy" software, bypassing traditional SaaS products by using AI to build custom internal solutions.  
* **New Economic Viability**: Historically, much desired software was too expensive to build using traditional methods (e.g., hardcoding complex business logic and edge cases). AI agents drastically lower this cost, making previously unviable software economically feasible to develop.

---

### **2\. The 4 Archetypes of Practical AI Agents**

We identified 4 highly actionable, low-hanging-fruit archetypes for AI agents that provide immediate business value without requiring cutting-edge, experimental infrastructure:

* **1\. Always Running (Support Agents)**:  
  * Often referred to as "Agent as a Service," this involves replacing or augmenting traditional 9-to-5 support roles with AI.  
  * Agents can operate 24/7 because they do not require sleep, offering continuous operational coverage.  
* **2\. Compresses the Research**:  
  * Many abstract business processes follow a specific pattern: *Business Event $\\rightarrow$ Research $\\rightarrow$ Human Decision*.  
  * Agents can instantly automate the "Research" phase.  
  * **Vercel Use Case**: When sales contact requests or abuse reports are submitted, agents instantly gather context (e.g., Googling the company, checking LinkedIn, assessing abuse validity). This reduces a 30-minute human research task to 5 minutes, vastly improving efficiency without altering the underlying process or risk profile.  
* **3\. Surfaces Hidden Information**:  
  * Companies possess vast amounts of latent data across issue trackers, Slack, and internal wikis.  
  * Agents can instantly surface this fragmented information to answer specific questions (e.g., "give me a list of updates"), a task that is practically impossible for a human to do manually in real-time.  
* **4\. The Boring Things (Job Satisfaction)**:  
  * Agents are ideal for automating the tasks that human workers despise the most.  
  * **Vercel Use Case**: Vercel built an in-house support agent that achieved a 90% ticket deflection rate. Consequently, the job satisfaction of the human support team skyrocketed because they were freed from mundane, repetitive issues (like rejected credit cards) and could focus on interesting, complex problems.

---

### **3\. The Shift in Software Architecture and Infrastructure**

The rise of AI agents necessitates a fundamental shift in how applications are designed, hosted, and secured.

* **Software is Now for Agents**: Agents are becoming the primary consumers of software. Over a 7-day period, 60% of the page views on Vercel.com were generated by AI agents rather than human users.  
* **UI/UX Evolution**: Because agents interact with software, traditional graphical dashboards are becoming less critical. Usage is shifting heavily toward APIs and Command Line Interfaces (CLIs), which agents can navigate more easily.  
* **Agent-Driven Infrastructure**: Infrastructure deployment needs to transition from human-driven processes to agent-driven processes. Agents must be able to write, deploy, and run code autonomously.  
* **Security by Default**: The current security paradigm (which Ubl likens to 1999\) is inadequate for autonomous agents. Future infrastructure must rely heavily on sandboxing (like Vercel's "Just Bash" tool) to safely execute agent-generated code.

---

### **4\. Strategic Outlook: The Application Layer vs. The Model Labs**

We conclude with a strategic forecast regarding the balance of power between foundational model creators (The Labs) and application builders (AI Engineers).

* **The Commoditization of Models**: The new application layer will thrive independently of specific foundational models. Foundational models will become commoditized as companies like Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic compete, ultimately driving the cost of intelligence down to a very low price floor.  
* **Value Accrual**: Because infrastructure and models will be cheap and commoditized, the actual business value will accrue to the application layer—the AI engineers building practical, business-specific agents.  
* **European Leadership**: We hope that Europe will act as a global leader in AI engineering and the application layer – with successful startups and projects originating from the region.
