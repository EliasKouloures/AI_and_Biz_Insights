# Survival Plan for the Age of AI: 6 Essential Human Skills
As AI becomes increasingly capable of "out-thinking" us in traditional ways, our value shifts from what we know to how we think, behave, and exist. To remain valuable, relevant, and hard to replace, we must focus on these six distinctly human skills.

***

### 1. Questioning
When AI makes answers cheap and abundant, **questions become the scarce and valuable resource.** Curiosity is the new killer app. The best minds are not answer-dispensers; they are unstoppable generators of interesting, unlikely questions ("What if?", "Why not?", "What are we actually trying to solve?").
*   **Actionable Technique: The "Five Whys"** 
    Before using an AI to generate solutions, ask "Why?" five times to drill down to the actual root of a problem. Often, the initial prompt you want to give an AI is solving the wrong problem. 
*   *Recommended Reading:* *The Book of Beautiful Questions* by Warren Berger.

### 2. Taste
AI is excellent at generating endless drafts, ideas, and "slop." **Taste—your discernment, judgment, and intuition—is the superpower that filters the meaningful from the mediocre.** The winning formula is not Human vs. Machine, but **Human + Machine**. AI provides the raw material; your taste shapes it into something real. 
*   **Actionable Technique: Create a "Hall of Fame"**
    Build a physical or digital folder (Notion, Dropbox, etc.) to collect examples of great writing, design, solutions, or innovations that resonate with you. Over time, studying this collection maps your taste, reveals patterns, and sharpens your standard of excellence.

### 3. Iteration
AI can accelerate the quantity of variations, but **human iteration delivers the quality.** Most good things start out bad. The magic isn't in the initial spark; it's in relentless, unglamorous revision—refining, redirecting, discarding, and polishing. 
*   **Actionable Techniques:**
    *   **The "Shitty First Draft":** Adopt Anne Lamott's principle. Don't aim for perfection; just get the first version done. 
    *   **Space Out Iterations:** Step away from your draft for a while. When you return with fresh eyes, flaws and obvious fixes will "pop."
    *   **The "Version 0.8 Rule":** Stop waiting until you are 100% ready. Share your work when it is 80% complete to force faster feedback, learning, and improvement.

### 4. Composition
AI is strong at delivering components (ingredients); **humans excel at assembling them (serving meals).** Composition is the artistic ability to synthesize ideas, arguments, or visuals into something coherent, meaningful, and emotionally resonant. Success comes from better arrangement, not just more pieces.
*   **Actionable Techniques:**
    *   **Rule of Three:** Whenever explaining a point, idea, or story, organize it into three distinct beats to make your communication clearer.
    *   **Learn to See Structure:** Turn on the 3x3 camera grid on your phone to practice the "Rule of Thirds." Place subjects on intersecting lines rather than dead center. 
    *   **The Movie Pause Game:** Pause visually striking movie scenes and analyze the composition—where are actors standing? How are they lit? Are there leading lines? 

### 5. Allocation
We are moving from a knowledge economy (compensated for what you know) to an **allocation economy (compensated for how well you deploy intelligence resources).** The future belongs to "Centaur Thinkers"—those who can orchestrate and allocate tools, teams, AI systems, and constraints toward a clear outcome.
*   **Actionable Techniques:**
    *   **Team Inventory:** List who (or what AI) performs best at which specific tasks, and delegate smarter.
    *   **The Two-Pile Technique:** Divide project tasks into Pile A for AI (speed, quantity, generation) and Pile B for You (real thinking, creativity, taste). Let AI handle Pile A so you can excel at Pile B. 
    *   **Time-as-Talent Audit:** Review your calendar for the last two weeks. Circle tasks that didn't require your unique creative skills and ask: *Could AI do this? Could a tool do this? Could someone else do this?* Stop managing time and start reallocating talent.

### 6. Integrity
Technology amplifies power, but ethics determines its usage. **Because AI scales power instantly and lacks a moral compass or conscience, human wisdom and integrity become infinitely more valuable.** Making choices based on honesty, fairness, responsibility, and accountability is the ultimate leadership skill.
*   **Actionable Techniques:**
    *   **The Washington Post Test:** Before taking action, ask yourself: *Would I be okay if this decision showed up on the front page of a major newspaper?* If no, hit the brakes.
    *   **Integrity Inversion:** Flip the scenario. Ask: *If someone did this to me, or to someone I love, would I think it was fair?* If no, don't do it. 

***
**The Core Philosophy:** When the world gets more artificial, we need to get more human.
