# Tony Robbins' 6-Step Decision-Making Framework (OOC-EMR)

## Core Philosophy
Make decisions based on clarity and structured thinking rather than emotions (e.g., anxiety, fear, excitement, or passion). The fundamental rule is to get the process **out of your head and onto paper** to manage the information effectively and reduce stress.

## The OOC-EMR Process

### 1. [O] Outcomes
* **Focus on Desires, Not Fears:** Start by defining exactly what you want, rather than what you are afraid of.
* **Prioritize:** Write down your desired outcomes and rank them in strict order of importance.
* **Define the 'Why':** Clarify the fundamental reasons behind your desired outcomes.

### 2. [O] Options
* **List Everything:** Write down every possible option available to you.
* **Avoid Tunnel Vision:** Do not lie to yourself by claiming there is only one option, as this artificially creates stress. Include even the options you do not currently favor.

### 3. [C] Consequences
* **Analyze Both Sides:** For every option listed, carefully document the potential upsides (benefits) and downsides (risks).
* **Be Comprehensive:** Map out the positive and negative outcomes for each path to the best of your current knowledge.

### 4. [E] Evaluate
* **Assess Probability:** Evaluate the realistic likelihood of each consequence actually occurring.
* **Avoid Extremes:** Do not waste energy stressing over a catastrophic downside with a near-zero probability, nor get overly optimistic about a massive upside with a near-zero probability. Focus on probable realities.

### 5. [M] Mitigate
* **Combine and Refine:** Look for ways to take positive elements from one option and mix them with another.
* **Reduce Risk:** Brainstorm specific strategies to mitigate or minimize the identified downsides. (Robbins notes there is almost always a way to do this).
* *Note:* Doing this visually on paper naturally shifts the brain from a state of fear and uncertainty into a state of creative problem-solving.

### 6. [R] Resolve
* **Make the Decision:** With all data clearly structured, evaluated, and mitigated, commit to your final choice.

## Stated Impact
Robbins claims this precise framework directly led to a personal $400 million financial gain from a single decision, alongside providing "priceless" emotional and spiritual clarity throughout his life.
