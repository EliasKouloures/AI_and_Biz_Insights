# CHEATSHEET: Embracing "Weird" AI
**Why the IT Department is Where AI Goes to Die**

---

### 🚨 THE CORE PROBLEM: "DE-WEIRDING" AI
Artificial Intelligence is fundamentally strange—it predicts words but also writes code, strategizes, and shows empathy. The biggest strategic mistake companies make is **treating AI like normal enterprise software** by trying to "de-weird" it.

* **The IT Mandate Mismatch:** IT departments exist to *minimize risk*. AI requires organizations to *embrace risk*, experiment wildly, and tolerate failure. Handing AI solely to IT is a category error.
* **The "Workslop" Effect:** Setting normal software KPIs (e.g., "90% adoption") results in employees generating endless, useless memos and PowerPoints just to hit metrics. 
* **The "Alien Paperweight":** Taking a transformative, unpredictable tool and using it merely to shave minutes off existing, mundane tasks.

---

### ⚖️ TWO PATHS: AUTOMATION vs. AUGMENTATION

| Feature | ❌ The Automation Trap (De-weirded) | ✅ The Augmentation Path (Weird) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mindset** | "How can we do the same things faster?" | "What new things are now possible?" |
| **Response to 30% Gains** | Fire 30% of the workforce. | Rebuild the organization around 100x output. |
| **Strategy Source** | Vendor demos, IT rollouts, Consultants. | Internal experimentation, genuine imagination. |
| **Ultimate Result** | Stagnation, layoffs, hidden employee usage. | New products, new markets, unlocked potential. |

---

### 💡 THE SOLUTION: THE 3-PART AI MODEL
To successfully integrate AI, avoid the standard IT rollout and adopt this three-tier framework:

#### 1. LEADERSHIP (Direction & Culture)
* **Do Not Delegate:** The CEO and senior managers must own the AI strategy, not middle management or IT.
* **Define the *What*:** Articulate how AI changes *what the organization is*, not just how it operates.
* **Lead by Example:** Leaders must use the systems themselves.
* **Create Safety:** Build incentives that make wild experimentation safe for employees.

#### 2. THE CROWD (Exploration & Expertise)
* **Unleash the Experts:** AI is most effective in the hands of domain experts (your employees).
* **Give Permission:** Provide access to AI tools and *genuine* permission to experiment.
* **Bottom-Up Discovery:** Trust the crowd to figure out use cases that even AI vendors haven't anticipated.

#### 3. THE LAB (Institutionalizing Knowledge)
* **Dedicated Team:** A mix of technical and non-technical employees working on generative AI *full-time*.
* **Core Mission:** Push boundaries, develop new workflows, and feed discoveries back into the organization.
* **Stop Flying Blind:** Build internal institutional knowledge instead of relying on conference keynotes.

---

### ⚠️ THE HIDDEN DANGER: SHADOW AI
When companies fail to incentivize AI properly, **employees rationally hide their AI use**. 

**Why they hide it:**
* **Fear:** They are afraid of punishment or making mistakes.
* **Distrust:** They believe productivity gains will lead to layoffs, not shared rewards.
* **Self-Interest:** They quietly finish work 90% faster and enjoy the free time.

**The Consequence:** An enormous information gap. Managers cannot see the true impact AI is having, making it impossible to develop a real, grounded strategy.

> **KEY TAKEAWAY**
> *There is no default good outcome with AI. Sanding down its strange edges guarantees a bad one. You cannot navigate strange territory by pretending your old maps will work.*
