# 📊 Forward Deployed Engineering (FDE) at Cursor

**Core Subject:** Structuring, scaling, and running a high-impact Forward Deployed Engineering function.

---

## 🎯 1. STRATEGIC POSITIONING: WHEN TO UTILIZE FDE
Determine FDE fit by mapping your customer base on a matrix of **Digital Maturity** vs. **Product Customization**:
*   **Low Maturity / Low Customization:** ❌ Traditional SaaS deployment. FDE is overkill.
*   **High Maturity / Low Customization:** ❌ Self-serve product with strong documentation. Not an FDE use case.
*   **High Maturity / High Customization:** ✅ FDE acts as a high-level advisor and adoption accelerator.
*   **Low Maturity / High Customization:** ✅ FDE acts as embedded transformation to guide them.
*   **Rule of Thumb:** Do not use FDEs for basic product training, staff augmentation, or writing bug tickets. Use them to extend the application and drive business outcomes.

## 🦄 2. THE "FDE UNICORN" PROFILE
The ideal FDE combines top-tier technical skills with high emotional/intellectual intelligence:
*   **Stakeholder Fluency:** Must operate smoothly across all levels, from CIOs/CTOs to developers.
*   **Change Agents:** Responsible for discovering use cases, understanding culture, and accompanying the customer through their digital transformation journey.
*   **Cutting-Edge Expertise:** Inherently curious and consistently up-to-date with rapid technological advancements.
*   **Feedback Conduit:** Operates delicately between the customer and internal Product/Engineering teams to inform the product roadmap.

## 🏗️ 3. FDE EXECUTION AT CURSOR
*   **Project-Based & Impactful:** Focus on strategic objectives driven by economic buyers/champions. Build solutions (like cloud agents or SDK integrations) that deliver strict ROI so the tool remains active after the FDE leaves.
*   **Pushing Edge Cases:** Expanding AI applications beyond standard Software Development Life Cycles (SDLC) into HR, Finance, and Supply Chain.
*   **Strict Co-Development:** Build *with* the customer in their codebase. Immediately reject "staff augmentation" requests to fill understaffed teams.
*   **Clear Mission:** Prevent scope creep with a formalized mission statement. *(Cursor's: "Partner with your organization to co-design and co-build your AI software factory.")*

## 👥 4. TEAM STRUCTURE & HIRING DYNAMICS
*   **Initial Hiring:** Start with senior, "unicorn" profiles (5+ years SWE experience) who are ready for customer-facing challenges (drawing from companies like Spotify, Rippling, Palantir).
*   **Matrix Organization:** Evolve structure over time. Start by **Geography**, then mature into **Industry-specific** teams (knowing banking or telco lingo establishes instant credibility).
*   **Subject Matter Experts (SMEs):** Assign deep domain experts for specific internal product areas (e.g., dedicated SMEs for long-running cloud agents or SDKs).

## 🚀 5. ACTIONABLE PLAYBOOK: RUNNING FDE PROJECTS
1.  **Validate the Root Problem:** Ensure you are solving the core issue, not a symptom. Speak directly to the internal workflow owner.
2.  **Define Success Upfront:** Establish strict baseline metrics before starting (e.g., "Reduce process from 3 hours to 20 minutes").
3.  **Keep Scope Directional:** Frame engagements around problem-solving phases (e.g., "6-week phase to automate X"). Avoid open-ended resourcing contracts ("Take 2 FDEs for 6 months"). This allows the team to pivot safely.
4.  **Involve the Customer Constantly:** Customers must actively participate in scoping, implementation, and human-in-the-loop validation. Never build alone in a silo.
5.  **Measure and Overcommunicate ROI:** Frame every outcome around one of three pillars: **Increasing Revenue**, **Decreasing Costs**, or **Mitigating Risk**.
6.  **Say "No" to Build Credibility:** Openly reject use cases where your tool isn't a fit.
7.  **Leverage Partners:** Offload heavy Change Management and scaled rollouts to System Integrators (SIs) and consulting partners.
8.  **Leave Artifacts Behind:** Ensure comprehensive documentation is handed over upon exit to guarantee sustainability.
